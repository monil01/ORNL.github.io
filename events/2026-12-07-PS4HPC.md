# Description
<p>
The PS4HPC workshop brings together the scientific community to discuss the
future of Programming Systems (PS) for High-performance Computing. This workshop
aims to foster discussion on funding directions, technical roadmaps, and community
synergies that will shape the next generation of PS for US Department of Energy's
scientific mission.
</p>

# Workshop Goals

<p>
The workshop aims to discuss and address priorities for the development and deployment of Programming Systems (PS) for High-performance Computing on the following topics:
</p>

- Funding directions for distributed PS, e.g., MPI implementations: OpenMPI and MPICH</li>
- Roadmap and synergies for ASCR's NGSST node-level portfolio of programming systems under S4PST: OpenMP, Kokkos, LLVM, Fortran, GASNet, Julia/JACC</li>
- PS needs from DOE HPC Applications and Facilities</li>
- Synergies with NGSST portfolio: PESO and STEP</li>
- The role of the High-performance Software Foundation (<a href="https://hpsf.io/">HPSF</a>) for the future of PS

# Topics of Interest

<p>
Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>MPI (OpenMPI and MPICH) for distributed programming systems</li>
  <li>Kokkos, OpenMP and LLVM development for node-level performance portability</li>
  <li>GASNet and PGAS models</li>
  <li>Legion and task-based runtime systems</li>
  <li>Fortran and modern HPC language features</li>
  <li>Julia/JACC for scientific computing</li>
  <li>Enzyme/AD and automatic differentiation</li>
  <li>Stewardship of programming systems for HPC</li>
</ul>
